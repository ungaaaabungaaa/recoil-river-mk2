# Product vision

Recoil River gives you a private place to collect web pages that matter to your work and reconnect them as your library grows. You decide which pages to keep. The product then gives each saved page a visual home and places it among related material.

The MVP joins a website, a Chrome extension, and a Convex backend. The website offers a visual library and an Obsidian-style mind map. The extension captures the current page through one deliberate action. Convex holds the shared account data and synchronizes changes in realtime.

Manual capture defines the product boundary. Recoil River does not observe browsing history. A save must finish before extraction, summarization, embedding, or discovery begins. Provider failures must leave the bookmark intact.

Saved material is private by default. The product must scope data to the signed-in user, give users a path to export and delete their records, respect source exclusions, and disclose third-party provider use. Those controls require later implementation and verification.

The product should feel like a dense field of useful evidence: black, white, grey, generous rounded surfaces, short labels, and technical line art. It should favor page context and relationships over dashboards and scores.
